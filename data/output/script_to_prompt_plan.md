# 0-to-0.5 Script-to-Prompt Generation Plan

This stage starts from the story script and generates a first prompt plan. It does not generate, replace, or overwrite any image or video asset.

## Story Script

# The Last Data

A future architectural data worker believes that scanning and uploading civilization data is meaningless routine work. In a cold data office, the protagonist is pressured by a boss to keep uploading architectural scan data even as an extinction-level impact alert interrupts the workday.

The conflict escalates because the boss still demands that the data upload be completed before evacuation. The protagonist finally breaks down, asking what the work is for if the world is ending.

After the catastrophe, the protagonist wakes in the ruins of the city. The office, the buildings, and the old civilization appear destroyed. In the ruins, the protagonist discovers a hard drive marked `CIVILIZATION ARCHIVE`.

When the hard drive activates, the stored architectural data becomes the basis for rebuilding the city. Blue holographic scans and architectural wireframes rise from the ruins, suggesting that the data once considered meaningless has become the last surviving blueprint of civilization.

## Ollama Generation Notes

Okay, here’s a Markdown prompt-generation plan for your AI video project, focusing on creating a detailed shot plan and initial prompting:

```markdown
## Story Logline:

A solitary architectural data worker confronts the implications of an impending extinction event when tasked with uploading crucial historical data – a civilization's blueprints – into a hard drive.  The escalating pressure forces him to question the purpose of this seemingly meaningless task, leading to a breakdown and a desperate search for meaning amidst devastation.


## Visual Continuity Bible

*   **Overall Tone:** Melancholic, sterile, slightly unsettling, with moments of stark beauty emerging from destruction.
*   **Color Palette:** Primarily cool tones – blues, grays, muted greens - reflecting the cold data office and the ruined city.  Hints of warm light (orange/yellow) will be used sparingly to represent hope or memory.
*   **Lighting:** Predominantly artificial lighting – harsh fluorescent lights in the office, flickering holographic displays, and deep shadows within the ruins.  Subtle blue glows emanating from the data drive.
*   **Camera Movement:** Slow, deliberate camera movements emphasizing isolation and scale of destruction. Utilize wide shots to establish the vastness of the ruined city, then zoom in on key details (the hard drive). Use tracking shots to follow the protagonist as he explores.
*   **Composition:** Rule of thirds – placing the protagonist slightly off-center to create a feeling of unease.  Use leading lines within the ruins to draw the viewer's eye toward the data drive.
* **Visual Style:** Photorealistic, with subtle digital artifacts and glitches in holographic projections to emphasize the artificiality of the data.

## Shot Plan (Initial Draft - 10-15 seconds)

1.  **Establishing Shot – Office Interior (0-3 seconds):** Wide shot of a sterile, dimly lit architectural data office. Focus on the protagonist working at a computer terminal, surrounded by stacks of old blueprints and holographic displays. The atmosphere is quiet but slightly oppressive.
2.  **Close Up - Protagonist’s Face (3-5 seconds):** Medium close up focusing on the protagonist's face – showing weariness and a hint of desperation. He looks at the hard drive with growing concern. 
3. **Medium Shot - Data Drive Activation (5-7 seconds)** A slow reveal as the hard drive activates, projecting holographic blueprints onto the walls of the office. The blue light intensifies slightly.
4.  **Tracking Shot – Protagonist’s Journey to Ruins (7-9 seconds):** Follow the protagonist walking through a partially collapsed hallway towards the ruins. 
5. **Medium Shot - Destruction of Cityscape (9-12 seconds)** Wide shot showcasing the city in ruins, emphasizing the scale and devastation.  Focus on crumbling buildings and overgrown vegetation.
6. **Close Up – Data Drive Fragment (12-14 seconds):** A close up of a fragment of the data drive, highlighting its complexity and the potential for rebirth. 
7. **Medium Shot - Protagonist’s Interaction with Data (14-17 seconds)** The protagonist reaches out to touch the data drive, revealing a small section of the blueprint – a single architectural element.
8.  **Wide Shot – Panoramic View of Ruins (17-20 seconds):** A sweeping wide shot showing the entirety of the ruined city, emphasizing its scale and emptiness. 


## Prompting Risks

*   **Overly Literal Interpretation:** Avoid overly literal interpretations of “destroyed” - focus on evocative imagery rather than a precise reconstruction.
*  **Lack of Emotional Depth:** Ensure the visuals don’t become too clinical or detached – maintain a sense of melancholy and unease.
* **Unclear Data Drive Details:** The data drive itself is crucial, so avoid generic representations. Focus on visual cues that suggest its significance (e.g., fragmented patterns, glowing elements). 
*   **Lack of Narrative Momentum:**  Maintain a deliberate pace – don’t rush the story progression. Let the visuals tell the story without relying solely on dialogue or narration.
* **Potential for Visual Fatigue:** The combination of bleakness and technological decay could be visually overwhelming - prioritize composition to guide the viewer's eye.


```
